Fried Rice

Description

Good old fried rice. As Uncle Roger put it, it's a very simple dish consisting of nothing more than "egg, fried and rice". I grew up eating it along the streets of Indonesia, and even now in Singapore, I find myself coming back to this stupidly delicious comfort food on a weekly basis. There's not much to learn about it: all you need to know is the basics, and you can personalise it to however you prefer!

Ingredients

  • Day-old cooked rice
  • Garlic
  • Shallots
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Soy Sauce
  • Fish Sauce
  • Shrimp Paste
  • Eggs
  • Chilli
  • MSG
  • Spring Onions

Steps

  1. Fry shallots in oil over high heat until super crisp and golden-brown, then transfer it to a bowl with a paper towel to drain off the oil.
  2. Fry an egg sunny side-up in oil over high heat until the bottom is super crisp, but the yolk is still relatively runny, then set the egg aside.
  3. Add a bit of oil, then add in your garlic, shrimp paste and chillis until the garlic turns golden-brown.
  4. Add in your soy sauce and fish sauce, mix all the wonderful flavours together, then toss in your day-old rice.
  5. Keep the wok moving and make sure to break up the rice. You want the beautiful individual grains to be seen. If you're feeling up to the task, practice your wok hei skills for an additional smokey flavour.
  6. Right before you finish cooking, add the spring onions in, then serve up on a plate with the fried shallots. Wallah, you've got your fried rice! Simple, isn't it?
